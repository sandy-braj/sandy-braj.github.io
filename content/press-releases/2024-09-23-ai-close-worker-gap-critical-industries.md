The research found Australia would need 1.68 million additional jobs over five years to meet projected GDP growth, creating a potential worker gap of up to 600,000 people. AI and emerging technologies were projected to help rebalance this deficit by saving time and shifting work toward higher-value tasks.

Pearson Director of Data Science Sandya Raj said: "In a world where people are living and working longer, and having multiple careers, insights from this latest Pearson research provides guidance on where individuals should focus their training, education, and learning. Employees and employers should look at how they can ride this wave of change by looking ahead, being prepared and using the best of AI and the best of human skills together."

The methodology used Faethm by Pearson's Labour Market Insights modelling to predict how jobs and industries will change over 1 to 15 years, considering emerging technology impact, economic growth, and labour market baselines. The model maps current task time within jobs against emerging technologies to estimate task, job, and industry-level shifts.
