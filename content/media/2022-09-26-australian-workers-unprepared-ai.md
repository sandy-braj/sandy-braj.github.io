Herald Sun article published on 26 September 2022.

The piece examined how many Australian workers remained underprepared for the workforce impact of automation and artificial intelligence, despite growing evidence that routine work would continue to shift.

Sandya Baratha Raj, Data Science Director at Faethm by Pearson, said many of the roles most exposed to automation, including sales assistants, bookkeepers, account clerks, and receptionists, were predominantly undertaken by women.

She said the advantage of this kind of workforce analysis was that affected roles could be identified early enough for workers and employers to respond proactively.

She advised workers to focus on transferable skills and move toward areas that would remain in demand, particularly roles where the human element would continue to matter.
