Effective workforce analysis, planning, and management require a deep understanding of the tasks and skills associated with different roles. This paper introduces a methodology for developing a comprehensive task framework that leverages large language models and large-scale job ads data.

The work proposes a task taxonomy design based on decomposition and reconstruction of tasks into a hierarchical structure using action-object pairings, refined with LLMs. It then integrates the taxonomy with occupation and skill linkages derived from job ads to align the framework with real-world workforce dynamics.

The paper demonstrates the framework through a visual analytics system for interactive exploration of tasks, occupations, and associated skills.
